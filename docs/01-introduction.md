# Introduction

The global financial ecosystem depends on the exchange of billions of messages every day between banks, payment processors, merchants, clearing systems, regulators, and customers.

Historically, financial institutions have relied on multiple messaging standards, many of which were developed decades ago and provide limited flexibility for modern digital payment ecosystems.

ISO 20022 was introduced to address these challenges by creating a universal language for financial messaging.

## Key Objectives

* Standardization
* Interoperability
* Rich Data Exchange
* Regulatory Compliance
* Operational Efficiency

## Benefits

### Better Customer Experience

More detailed payment information improves transparency.

### Enhanced Compliance

Supports anti-money laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) initiatives.

### Improved Automation

Reduces manual intervention and payment exceptions.

### Global Interoperability

Enables consistent communication across payment networks worldwide.
